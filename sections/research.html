My Research Areas

Conservation Biology

Focuses on protecting species, their habitats, and ecosystems from extinction and degradation. This involves understanding ecological processes, human impacts, and developing strategies for sustainable coexistence.

Behavioral Ecology

Explores how animal behavior is shaped by ecological and evolutionary pressures. Research in this area often examines foraging strategies, mating systems, social interactions, and predator-prey dynamics.

Climate Change Ecology

Investigates the impacts of climate change on ecosystems, species distributions, phenology, and community structures. This includes studying adaptation, vulnerability, and potential mitigation strategies.

Urban Ecology

Studies the interactions between humans and natural systems in urban environments. This interdisciplinary field examines biodiversity in cities, urban planning for sustainability, and the ecological roles of urban green spaces.

Restoration Ecology

Dedicated to assisting the recovery of ecosystems that have been degraded, damaged, or destroyed. It involves applying ecological principles to restore biodiversity, ecosystem functions, and resilience.

Wildlife Disease Ecology

Examines the dynamics of diseases in wild animal populations, including their causes, transmission, and impacts on host populations and ecosystem health. This often involves epidemiology, immunology, and conservation aspects.
